Maintaining relationship with Service Providers

 

1. On-site visits. An on-site visit is critical to successful planning. This is important if the service provider is new to the site or if the customer's tasks not previously performed by that contractor. 

2. A detailed review of the scope of the requested work. Both parties need to review all the steps involved in accomplishing the project's scope. This may sound elementary, but if omitted, the results are frequently undesirable. Having a detailed, step-by-step plan that both parties understand ensures the expected results. 

3. Upfront discussion of quality expectations. Each customer has different expectations for quality, and it is critical for them to communicate detailed quality expectations to the contractor.

4. Project-specific safety planning. Getting the job done safely is of mutual and vital importance. Customer and Service Provider should agree on the scope-specific safety elements. Changes in project conditions or scope are reasons to revisit safety planning. 

5. Establishing a point of contact for both parties. This is the first step in creating rapport and relationship building and ensures successful communications throughout the project. A designated point of contact improves the interactions between the service provider and the customer.

6. Timelines. An agreed project timeline is valuable for both parties. The customer can plan his future and the contractor can provide an accurate cost estimate, as well as honor other commitments. 

7. Clarifying payment terms and conditions. Both parties need to agree on payment terms and conditions. Before the project, the service provider should provide an agreed fee for additional work or unanticipated changes in scope. This prevents misunderstandings and promotes a strong relationship between customer and service provider.
