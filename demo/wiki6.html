Continous Inspections

 

Your new home should be inspected periodically during the course of construction. The idea is to catch as many potential problems as possible before construction is finished, though some issues may not surface until you’ve lived in the home for a period of time.

Talk to your service provider early on about attending inspections. Even if your presence is not required, it’s an opportunity to learn more about what’s behind the walls of your new home and how everything works. If you’re planning to hire your own service provider to do an additional review of the home, notify your service provider prior to the start of construction.

For safety as well as logistical reasons, service providers discourage customers from dropping in unannounced at the construction site. If you’d like to pay a visit, be sure to arrange it in advance. Chances are your service provider will conduct regular walkthroughs to bring you up to speed on the progress of the work.
